    Submitting a Transaction

    Click the Transactions link in the menu, then click Add. Identify the two members making the trade (one of them should be you, unless you're logged in as an admin), by entering their member ID's in the spaces provided, and the amount. These three fields are required - in other words, you can't enter a trade without specifying an amount and who the two trading members are. Optionally, you can enter a description of the trade. The Submitted Via field identifies where you're logged in from, and is there for your protection. It provides a possible audit trail in case someone is trying to impersonate you and submit transactions against your account.

    Using Keyword Fragments

    You can use keyword fragments to search as well. For instance, using ax as a keyword will return matching ads that offer saxaphones as well as tax preparation (since both contain the keyword fragment ax). Similarly, entering 678 in the phone field will pull up all ad listings where 678 is somewhere in the phone number. This can be useful if you can't quite remember a person's phone number. Other search tricks include: entering .org under email will return ads from members whose email indicates they're a non-profit group. In the next section you'll learn of a trick to find all product websites, and how to use the LETS webtool to promote your site.
     
    Promoting Your Websites

    As we saw above, some keyword tricks can be used to create lists of matching ads. The same technique can be applied to find product websites. For instance, by using the keyword http under the URL field, you'll generate a list of all goods and services that also have webpages associated with them. Typically this is a website describing the product or service, or it could be a personal webpage. When you create your ad listings, you have the option of entering a link this way in the URL field (remember to type the full URL, though - the software won't try and guess that you meant "http://www.somesite.com", if you merely enter "somesite.com"), so that prospective customers that are browsing your online LETS can follow the link from your ad to webpages you've designed, to read more about you, your company, or one of your products. Any text you enter in the URL field is assumed to be a web link, and the software will automatically show it as a clickable link.
     
    Cross-System Searches

    The software is designed to allow many LETS within a region to interact online, providing a wider range of goods and services available to their members (conversely, it also allows members to advertise their services over a wider area). If you can't find what you're looking for within your own LETS, you may want to search the ad listings of members in neighbouring towns (note this depends on whether your regional LETS have opted to use the software this way). If your LETS is intertrading regionally, remember to check the upper right-hand corner of each ad listing to see which system the ad was submitted in.     Creating Login Access for Members

    One of the most common tasks you'll perform as a LETS admin (using this online system), is to create your member logins. Without them your members cannot use the system. The process is actually very easy: simply click the Members link (which appears in the menu only for you, since your login has administrative permissions), and you'll see a screen like this:

    This screen is used for some core administrative functions, including:

    Creating New Member Logins:

      When a new member joins your LETS and also has internet access, you can include them in the online system by filling out the Create New Username and Password fields, and then hitting the Update/Create User button, as shown above. (Remember to send them an email informing them of their new login, and the URL of the site!).

      If you try to create a username that already exists, the software will stop you with a Try Again message in red, otherwise a successful Login Created message. To avoid duplicating an existing login, you can scan the Existing Members drop-down list to see which usernames are already taken. The software needs a unique username for every member, even across different LETS within your intertrading group. For this reason we suggest adding a prefix to your member logins, when you create them - ie. Account 503 becomes "Sydney_503", as a username. This naming practice is only necessary if your LETS intertrades (or will in the future), and its account ID's might overlap with other LETS groups in your region.

    Editing Existing Logins:
      Select the login you want to edit from Existing Members and hit Inquire. Their permissions, password and username will be retrieved to-screen, ready for you to edit. Typically you would use this feature to restrict their permissions if their membership lapses (ie. remove their permission to Add, Modify or Delete ad listings, until they've paid their member dues), or to change a member's password.     Publishing the Ad Directory Listings

    Although the webtool lets your members surf the ad listings online, this doesn't benefit your members who may not have internet access. For that reason we recommend that you download your system's ad listings and publish them also as a paper version, for inclusion in the normal (paper) ad directory that you distribute to your members. As of this writing you can print the web version of your ad directory directly from your browser, using the following steps:
    • Click Ad Directory if it's not the current database in use (ie. if some other table
      name is shown in the menu's white square). This loads the ads database.
    • Click Search to call up the search screen.
    • Select your LETS from the System drop-down.
    • Select all from the Ad Category drop-down.
    • Select all from the Ad Type drop-down.
    • Leave all other keyword fields blank, except for the sorting options near the bottom of the screen. Here you want to set Hits Per Page to a very high value, like 99999. This ensures you'll get every ad from your system returned in the Search Results.
    • Set Sort By to Ad_Category. This will ensure that all the ads appear alphabetically by category name, making it easier for members to lookup services on paper. Unfortunately, the Wanted and Offered ads will appear mixed together within those categories - ultimately I'll fix that!
    • Hit the Search button, and the screen will show the entire web directory for your LETS, sorted by category. To print it, go to your web browser's menu and select File and then Print (you may want to choose Page Setup first, to make sure it's set for paper size you're going to use.
    The resulting printed directory is not very customizable - it looks exactly the way it appears on-screen within your web browser. However, I'll be providing an option to download your system's raw data in a format you can use to import into desktop publishing or database software.
     
    Downloading Your System's Raw Data

    This screen is available only to LETS admins, for the purpose of polishing the printed output they send to members. Typically a LETS will publish a printed Directory of Goods and Services, as well as Transaction Statements that they provide for members, summarizing their various trading. The Download System Data screen is used to grab the raw data their members have submitted online, in a file format that can be readily imported into Word, Wordperfect, Access, Quattro Pro, or any commercially available desktop publishing package. The data files you download are text-delimited database files, called ads.zip and trades.zip. They're not really zipfiles (ie. they're not compressed), but that file extension is used to force your browser to initiate the download. Once you have both files, you can simply rename them with a .txt extension, (since they're really just sorted textfiles), after which you can readily import them into whatever publishing software you're using. The most common and easiest use of this data is to simply import it into Access or Quattro Pro - two common database tools, then printing them up using a report template you've pre-designed using those tools. Depending on how many LETS want to go online with the webtool system, I can expand this utility to provide a canned Rich Text Format for the sorted output, essentially a canned and formatted document that's ready to go for printing. This would eliminate any extra steps you'd have to take in formatting the data, since I would essentially automate that for you. Alternatively you can simply print the Directory Listings directly from your browser, using the steps outlined in Publishing the Ad Directory Listings.

    Do I need to Abandon My Current Software?

    No. The webtool only handles ads and trades submitted through the web, and won't show any that are submitted through your regular software. And since many LETS members do not have internet access, we recommend printing the web version of your system's ad listings and including it as an insert in your regular paper directory, for the benefit of members who are not web-connected. Note that the webtool currently does not tally account balances, or provide utilities for charging member fees of any kind. At this stage, our honest assessment is that the software can provide a complement to your existing software, but not replace it completely. What it will do is alleviate much of your workload as a LETS admin, since members themselves can manage their own ad listings, relieving you and your staff of having to do that data-entry. It will also eliminate the reliance on voice mail (and having to constantly rewind those messages, to transcribe them for data-entry). The software will also provide up-to-the-minute listings of goods and services, rather than a once-monthly printed directory that gets progressively outdated as you approach month-end.
